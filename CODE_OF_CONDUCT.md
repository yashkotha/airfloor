# Code of Conduct

## The short version

AirFloor is a small open-source project about air quality in India, maintained by one person. Be kind, be patient, and remember there is a human on the other end of every issue and pull request.

## Expected behaviour

- Be respectful and welcoming, especially to first-time contributors.
- Give feedback on the work, not the person.
- Assume good intent. English is a second language for many of us here, so read charitably.
- Keep discussions on topic and constructive.
- Accept that the maintainer has limited time and may say no.

## Unacceptable behaviour

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks of any kind.
- Discrimination based on caste, religion, gender, sexuality, language, region, disability, or anything else.
- Sexualised language or imagery.
- Publishing someone's private information without their permission.
- Trolling, deliberate derailing, or sustained bad-faith arguing.

## Scope

This applies in all project spaces: the GitHub repository, issues, pull requests, discussions, and anywhere you are representing AirFloor in public.

## Enforcement

I maintain this project alone. If someone is behaving badly, open a GitHub issue. If the matter is sensitive, email me instead, the address is on my GitHub profile. I read every report, keep things as private as I can, and aim to respond within a few days. Consequences range from a warning to being blocked from the project, and I will always explain the decision.

If I am the problem, tell me directly. I mean it.
